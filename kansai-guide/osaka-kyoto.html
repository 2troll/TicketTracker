2-Day Itinerary

The complete Kansai arc

The best of both cities, back-to-back — no overlap, no repetition, maximum contrast.

Day 1 — Osaka
8:30 AM
Hotel Pickup — Osaka
I meet you at your hotel lobby. No station confusion, no navigating the subway on day one. We walk into Osaka together.
Hotel Pickup Included
9:15 AM
Kuromon Market — The Kitchen of Osaka
200-year-old covered market. We arrive early: vendors setting up, almost no other tourists. Wagyu skewers, sea urchin, giant scallops grilled in front of you. I know which stalls use the best ingredients and which ones are tourist traps.
Food Tasting Included
11:00 AM
Shinsekai & Tsūtenkaku Tower
The retro working-class neighbourhood built to look like Paris in 1912. Kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers on checkered tablecloths), billiards halls, old men playing shōgi. Not for every traveller — absolutely unforgettable for those who appreciate real neighbourhood character.
2:00 PM
Osaka Castle & Surroundings
The castle that Toyotomi Hideyoshi built in 1583 — seat of the daimyo who first unified Japan. The moat, the white towers, the golden shachi ornaments. I skip the interior museum and take you to the hidden garden on the east side where locals picnic, and the view from the north tower wall is better than the main gate.
7:00 PM
Dotonbori Neon & Night Izakaya
The canal comes alive after dark. We do the full circuit: neon Glico man, the 3D crab, the rotating sushi counter at the end of the alley. Then: a standing izakaya — yakitori skewers, cold Sapporo draft, locals coming in after work. Osaka at its most alive.
Best Sake Pairing of the Trip
Day 2 — Kyoto
8:00 AM
Train to Kyoto — 15 Minutes
JR Shinkansen or Shinkansen-speed Haruka — Osaka to Kyoto in 15 minutes. We arrive when the temples open but before the tour buses.
8:30 AM
Arashiyama — Bamboo & River
The bamboo grove before 9 AM is something else entirely — light filtering through 20-metre stalks, the sound of wood creaking in wind. Then the Ōi River, the wooden boats, the Tenryūji garden with its pond and raked gravel design that hasn't changed since 1345.
11:30 AM
Hidden Nishiki Market Lunch
Kyoto's narrow covered food street: pickled vegetables, fresh yuba (tofu skin), grilled mochi, matcha soft serve. We eat standing up, the Kyoto way. Lunch costs about ¥800 and is better than any restaurant.
Street Food Lunch
2:00 PM
Gion & Private Tea Ceremony
The preserved geisha district — stone lanes, wooden machiya townhouses, hanging lanterns. Then a private matcha tea ceremony in a back-street chashitsu with a practitioner who has studied for 20 years. Not a tourist performance — an actual ceremony.
Tea Ceremony Included
5:30 PM
Sake Tasting on the Return Train
I board the return train with a selection of 4 single-brewery sake purchased in Kyoto. We taste on the 15-minute ride back — the most comfortable sake bar in Kansai. You arrive at Osaka station with the day complete, slightly happy, and already planning to return.
