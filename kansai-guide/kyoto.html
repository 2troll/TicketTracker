Detailed Itinerary

Your Kyoto day

We time every stop to miss the tour bus crowds — arriving before them, leaving before they arrive.

8:30 AM
Kyoto Station — Central Exit
Meet at the statue outside the central exit. I'll have coffee. We take the JR line south — 5 minutes — to Fushimi.
9:00 AM
Fushimi Inari — Before the Crowds
10,000 vermilion torii gates up a forested mountain. At 9 AM the first tour buses haven't arrived. We walk the inner path that 90% of visitors skip. At the top: Kyoto spread below, no noise, no selfie sticks.
Best Photo Opportunities
11:00 AM
Philosopher's Path — Silence Walk
A 2 km canal path lined with cherry trees, connecting Ginkakuji to Nanzenji. Named after a philosopher who walked it daily. We walk it slowly. I point out the small neighbourhood shrines tourists walk past without noticing.
12:30 PM
Lunch — Local Kaiseki or Ramen
Your choice: a 5-course kaiseki lunch at a restaurant locals use (no English menu — I order for you), or an old-school ramen-ya that's been in the same family for 60 years.
Reservation Arranged in Advance
2:30 PM
Gion — Secret Alleys & Tea Ceremony
Not the main Hanamikoji street — the side lanes behind it. Preserved machiya townhouses, private tea rooms. We enter a small chashitsu for a private matcha tea ceremony with a practitioner who studied for 15 years.
Tea Ceremony Included
5:00 PM
Riverside Dinner — Kamo River
Kyoto's restaurant terraces open over the Kamo river in summer. In winter, warm nabe stew at a counter restaurant that seats 8. Either way, the best possible end to the day.
Dinner Reservation Included
