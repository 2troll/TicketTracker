Detailed Itinerary

Your step-by-step day

Every stop is timed to avoid crowds and hit each place at its best moment.

9:00 AM
Meet at Namba Station — Exit 14
We meet at street level. No busy lobby waiting — straight into the city. I'll brief you on the day and we walk.
9:20 AM
Kuromon Market — Opening Hour
200-year-old market hall. We arrive as vendors set up — far fewer tourists, vendors talk to us. Samples of wagyu skewers, fresh uni, grilled oysters. You eat, I explain.
Food Tasting Included
10:45 AM
Dotonbori Canal Walk
The famous neon strip — but we hit it from the canal level, not the tourist bridge. Glico man, takoyaki at the original Aiduya shop. I show you the angles locals photograph.
12:30 PM
Shinsekai — Retro Neighbourhood Lunch
Built to look like Paris and NYC in 1912, now a beloved working-class district. Lunch at a kushikatsu counter: deep-fried skewers, cold Asahi, Tsūtenkaku Tower view.
Lunch Stop
2:30 PM
Hidden Shotengai Shopping Arcade
A covered arcade untouched since the 1970s. Local snack shops, vinyl record stores, a grandmother selling handmade mochi. The Osaka tourists never see.
4:00 PM
Standing Sake Bar — End of Day
A tiny tachizake bar: standing room, three stools, 40 different sake labels chalked on a board. The owner selects your pour. Perfect way to close Osaka.
