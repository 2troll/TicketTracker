Detailed Itinerary

Your Kobe afternoon

Kobe is compact — four completely different worlds within walking distance of each other.

10:00 AM
Sannomiya Station — Start
20 minutes from Osaka by Hanshin Express. We meet at the east exit. Kobe is immediately different from Osaka — calmer, wider streets, a European elegance that comes from 150 years of international trade.
10:20 AM
Kitano Ijinkan — The Foreign Quarter
When Japan opened to the world in 1868, foreign merchants built mansions on this hill above the port. Victorian, Moorish, Tudor — they're all here, preserved exactly as they were. We visit the English House and the German Consulate. The view of Osaka Bay from the top is extraordinary.
Best View in Kansai
12:00 PM
Kobe Wagyu — Teppanyaki Lunch
Kobe beef is the original wagyu — raised in Hyogo Prefecture, graded A5, marbled like nothing else on earth. We eat at a teppanyaki counter: the chef cooks in front of you, slices A5 sirloin tableside. One bite is the reason people fly to Japan. I pre-book the counter — it fills weeks in advance.
Wagyu Lunch Reservation Included
2:00 PM
Nada District — Sake Brewery Tasting
Kobe has been Japan's most important sake-producing region since the Edo period. The Miyamizu water from Rokko Mountain makes it exceptional. We visit one of the historic breweries in Nada — cedar casks, fermentation rooms, and a tasting of 8 different sake with seasonal pairings selected by the toji (master brewer).
8-Sake Tasting Session
4:00 PM
Meriken Park — Harbour at Golden Hour
The waterfront where Kobe's international story began. The Kobe Port Tower in red steel, the maritime museum, and the sea stretching to Awaji Island. We watch the light change on the water. The whole city glows. Perfect close to a perfect day.
