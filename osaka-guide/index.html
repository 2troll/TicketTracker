The Kansai Region · 関西地方

Where Japan's
history was born

Kansai is Japan's cultural and spiritual core. Home to two ancient capitals, the country's finest cuisine, and a way of life that remains fiercely distinct from Tokyo — warmer, louder, more honest.

Seven prefectures. Centuries of stories. Knowing where to look — and who to ask — makes all the difference.

大阪

Osaka

Japan's kitchen. Street food, neon, merchant spirit, and the most open-hearted people you'll meet anywhere in the country.

京都

Kyoto

A thousand years of imperial culture. Wooden temples, bamboo groves, geisha districts — and the hidden spots tourists rarely find.

奈良

Nara

Ancient deer roam freely among UNESCO shrines. Japan's first permanent capital — serene, underrated, unforgettable.

神戸

Kobe

Port city elegance, legendary beef, and a European-influenced skyline nestled between mountains and sea.

姫路

Himeji

Japan's most perfect castle — white as a heron in flight, unchanged for four centuries, worth every kilometre.

高野山

Kōyasan

Sleep in a mountain monastery. Walk lantern-lit cemetery paths at dawn. A world completely apart.
